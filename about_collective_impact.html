Change the System

Collective Impact’s aim is to intervene and reorient the set of behaviors, interactions, projects and programs in an existing system in order to produce a different, enduring and large-scale result.

[caption]

The Five Conditions of Collective Impact[/caption]

Collective Impact is a more disciplined and higher performing approach for building cross-sector partnerships that leads to positive and consistent progress at scale against the issues we are most passionate about. Complex social problems, such as the health of returning citizens, and their solutions arise from the interaction of many organizations within a larger system. The ultimate goal is to sustain and scale long-term systemic change and impact in the community.

“When human beings gather in groups, a depth of awareness and insight, a transcendent knowing, becomes available to us that, if accessed, can lead to profound action. We call this transcendent knowing collective wisdom. This knowing is not of the mind alone, nor is it of any individual alone. When knowing and sense of right action emerges, it does so from deep within the individual participants, from within the collective awareness of the group, and from within the larger field that holds the group.”

- John Ott

For a much more detailed look at the Collective Impact approach, please check out John Kania & Mark Kramer's "Collective Impact" essay published by the Stanford Social Innovation Review in their Winter 2011 issue.
